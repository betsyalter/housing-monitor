# Step 3 — Value Chain (Where Dollars Flow When Housing Turns Over)

> _Status: 2026-05-01. Default-driven first draft. ASSUMPTION-flagged
> on per-transaction dollar estimates and aftermarket spend share._

The structural map (Step 1) catalogs *who* captures dollars at each
stage of a housing transaction. The value-chain analysis quantifies
*how much* and *when* — translating "Factor 1 unwind" into specific
revenue dollars per subsector at specific lags.

The exercise: trace $1 of existing-home transaction volume through the
ecosystem, then aggregate to the thesis-scale (5.3M annualized
existing-home sales = "normalized" pace; current 4.0M).

---

## The transaction snapshot — $400K existing home

A median US existing-home transaction at 2026 prices [JCHS 2025
median $412,500; FHFA HPI Q1 2026 $403K — using $400K as round
working figure].

### At-closing dollar flow

| Stage | $ captured | % of price | Subsector |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Brokerage commission (split buyer/seller) | $20,000-$24,000 | 5-6% | Layer 1 — brokers (RMAX, RDFN, etc.) |
| Title insurance + escrow | $2,000-$4,000 | 0.5-1% | Layer 5 — title (FAF, FNF, STC) |
| Mortgage origination fee | $2,000-$8,000 | 0.5-2% | Layer 2 — originators (RKT, UWMC, etc.) |
| Inspection + appraisal | $700-$1,500 | <0.5% | Layer 8 (smaller cos, mostly private) |
| Recording + transfer tax | $1,000-$4,000 | 0.25-1% | (state revenue, not capturable) |
| **At-closing total** | **$25,700-$41,500** | **6.5-10%** | |

### Post-closing dollar flow (90-day mover-cohort spend)

| Stage | $ captured | Subsector |
|---|---:|---|
| Furnishings (mattress, sofa, dining, beds) | $4,000-$8,000 | Layer 6 — W, RH, RST, LZB, SNBR |
| Appliances (refrigerator, washer/dryer, kitchen) | $2,000-$5,000 | Layer 6 — WHR, GNRC |
| Home improvement (paint, fixtures, minor renovation) | $2,000-$6,000 | Layer 6 — HD, LOW |
| Moving services (truck, labor, storage) | $1,500-$5,000 | Layer 8 — UHAL, LSI |
| HVAC service / replacement | $300-$2,000 | Layer 6 — WSO, LII |
| Yard / outdoor (lawn, pool startup) | $300-$3,000 | Layer 6 — POOL, LESL, SITE |
| **Post-closing total** | **$10,100-$29,000** | |

### Annual carry costs (Layer 7 + ongoing Layer 6)

| Stage | $ captured | Subsector |
|---|---:|---|
| Property insurance (annual) | $1,500-$4,000+ | Layer 7 — ALL, TRV, PGR |
| PMI (if applicable, year 1) | $1,500-$4,000 | Layer 7 — MTG, ESNT, RDN |
| HVAC maintenance / service contracts | $200-$600 | Layer 6 |
| Routine repair / handyman | $1,000-$3,000 | Layer 6 — HD, LOW |

**Total per-transaction value capture (closing + 90-day + year-1
annual carry):** ~$40,000-$75,000 across the eight functional layers
on a $400K transaction.

## At thesis scale — 5.3M (normalized) vs 4.0M (current)

Current pace: **4.06M existing-home sales in 2024** [JCHS 2025 p. 1] —
30-year low. Trailing 4-week 2026 SAAR ~3.98M [housing_context.json].

Normalized pace: **5.3M average 2015-2019** [NAR existing-home sales
historical].

**Delta from unlock: 1.3M incremental transactions per year** at
normalized pace. With per-transaction value capture of ~$50K (median),
that's:

```
1.3M transactions × $50K per transaction = ~$65B incremental annual
revenue across the housing-services + aftermarket layers
```

Distributed by layer (using the per-transaction dollar shares):

| Layer | Δ annual revenue | Tickers (disproportionate share) |
|---|---:|---|
| Brokerage | ~$25-30B | RMAX, RDFN, COMP, EXPI, OPEN |
| Title insurance | ~$3-5B | FAF, FNF, STC, ORI |
| Mortgage origination | ~$3-10B (highly variable) | RKT, UWMC, COOP, PFSI, LDI |
| Furnishings | ~$5-10B | W, RH, RST, LZB, SNBR, LEG |
| Home improvement | ~$3-8B | HD, LOW |
| Appliances | ~$2-6B | WHR, GNRC |
| Moving / storage | ~$2-6B | UHAL, LSI |
| Other aftermarket | ~$2-5B | Various Layer 6 |
| **Total** | **~$50-80B** | |

The math of normalization: a return to 2015-2019 pace creates
~$50-80B/yr of incremental industry revenue. **For context, that's
roughly 0.3% of US GDP — modest in macro terms but large in
single-industry terms.** It's also the math behind the manual's
"trough → mid-cycle EBITDA expansion" inning marker for cyclical
housing names [Investment Research Training Manual v5.0, L1539].

## Per-subsector beta to volume normalization

The dollars distribute differently across subsectors because of
revenue-mix concentration:

| Subsector | Revenue from existing-home volume | Beta to unlock |
|---|---:|---|
| Title insurance | 90-95% | **Very high** (binary) |
| Real estate brokers | 95%+ | **Very high** (commission) |
| Mortgage origination (purchase) | 80-90% | **Very high** (refi cycle adds further leverage) |
| Manufactured housing builder (entry-level) | 80-90% | **High** |
| Tier-1 homebuilder | 60-70% | **High** (new + existing partial substitutes) |
| Furnishings (high-end) | 30-50% | **Medium-high** (mover cohort + Pent-up) |
| Home improvement retail | 10-15% | **Medium** (mover cohort fraction) |
| Appliances | 25-40% | **Medium** |
| Insurance (P&C) | 5-10% | **Low** |
| PMI | 60-80% | **High** |
| Lumber / building products | 30-50% | **Medium-high** |

**The pure-play volume-beta names (90-95% revenue from transactions)
are the highest-conviction longs in a Factor 1 unwind.** Title
insurance (FAF, FNF, STC, ORI) and brokerage (RMAX, RDFN, COMP) sit at
the top. Mortgage origination (RKT, UWMC, PFSI) is close behind with
the additional refi-cycle leverage.

The lower-beta names (HD/LOW, P&C insurance) are diversified-moat
businesses where the unlock contributes ~10-30% of revenue lift but is
not the load-bearing thesis. They're appropriate as portfolio breadth
but not pure thesis vehicles.

## Lag structure (the transmission timeline)

Per Investment Research Training Manual v5.0 (L817): each transmission
channel must be modeled separately because each lags differently. Here
is the cascade applied to housing:

```
Trigger event: 30yr falls 25 bps (e.g., from 6.30% to 6.05%)

  T+0       Refi applications spike (MBA Wednesday print)
  T+1-2w    Mortgage origination revenue (Layer 2) — refi 
            component shows in sequential rev
  T+4-6w    Pending home sales tick up (NAR monthly)
  T+8-12w   Existing-home sales (NAR monthly) print rises
            Title insurance premium volume tracks closing 
            count (Layer 5) 
  T+8-16w   Builder net orders rise (Tier-1 builder 8-Ks)
  T+12-24w  Brokerage gross commission income rises 
            (Layer 1 quarterly)
  T+16-26w  Builder closings rise (Tier-1 builder 8-Ks)
  T+24-36w  Aftermarket spend (mover cohort) rises 
            (Layer 6 quarterly comps) 
  T+30-50w  PMI new policies (Layer 7) rise; 
            insurance new-business premium follows
  T+50-100w REIT NOI inflection (Layer 4) — apartment 
            short side starts to underperform
```

**Implication for positioning sequence.** A position-build that
follows the lag structure scales risk over 12-18 months:

1. **Initial position (T-0 to T+12 weeks):** Layer 2 (mortgage
   originators), Layer 1 (brokers), Layer 5 (title insurers).
   Pure-play volume-beta names.
2. **Second tranche (T+12 to T+30 weeks):** Layer 3 (builders +
   suppliers). Order-cycle confirmation gives second-derivative
   confidence.
3. **Third tranche (T+24 to T+50 weeks):** Layer 6 (aftermarket).
   Mover-cohort spend confirmed in HD/LOW comps.
4. **Short side (T+30 to T+100 weeks):** Layer 4 apartment REIT
   short basket, scaled through the entry-trigger framework in the
   apartment basket file.

## Counter-thesis on the value chain

The strongest version of the bear case on Step 3:

**(1) The dollar capture has structurally compressed.** Brokerage
commissions face downward pressure from the NAR 2024 antitrust
settlement (buyers' agents may not be paid by sellers in many
markets, splitting the 5-6% in new ways). Mortgage origination
margins have compressed since 2020 as fintech entrants (Better.com,
Tomo, etc.) and price-war competition reduced gain-on-sale spreads.
Title insurance has faced regulatory scrutiny on its loss-ratio
margins. **The 5-10% of transaction value captured by
services-and-products may be 4-7% in 2026-2028, reducing the
incremental revenue per transaction.**

**(2) Aftermarket spend is being absorbed by used / secondary
channels.** The "mover spends $10-15K on furnishings in 90 days" was
true in 2015-2020. The 2024-2026 environment shows aftermarket
spending shifting toward used / Marketplace / Craigslist channels for
furniture and appliances, reducing the share captured by RH/W/WHR/
SNBR. The aftermarket layer's beta may be lower than historical.

**(3) Lag-structure compression.** Modern refi processes (e-mortgage,
hybrid digital closing) have shortened the rate-trigger-to-closing
timeline. T+8-12 weeks for closings may be T+5-8 weeks in 2026. This
tightens the thesis but also tightens the position-building window.

**Counter to counter-thesis.** (1) is real but symmetric — as commission
revenue per-transaction shrinks, transaction count rises, and the
mathematics of operating leverage on title insurance and originators
holds even with margin compression. (2) is a research item; the
secondary-channel spend share has not been quantified at scale and may
turn out to be smaller than perceived. (3) is favorable to the thesis
(faster transmission = earlier confirmation = lower sequence risk).
