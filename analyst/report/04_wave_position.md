# Step 4 — Wave Position

> _Status: 2026-05-01. Default-driven first draft. ASSUMPTION-flagged
> on cycle-position call and inning marker._

The cycle-position read is the single most important call in this
report. **Get the wave position wrong and the entry timing is wrong;
get the entry timing wrong and conviction names become value traps**
[per Investment Research Training Manual v5.0, L1284-1286 on
structural-vs-cyclical misclassification].

The wave-catching framework [Manual L1400-1408]: see the wave coming
(lead indicators); catch the wave (position before financial momentum
confirms); ride the wave (hold through recognition phase); get off as
it crests. This section uses the Industry Repository three-layer
structure [Manual L2460-2476] to locate where in the cycle housing
sits today.

Combining all three layers:

**Direction (Layer 1):** Ambiguous — spot easing, forward tightening.
**Position (Layer 2):** Trough on volume, ceiling on affordability.
**Valuation (Layer 3):** Above-trough on most thesis names; trough on
some stressed.

_ASSUMPTION (Wyatt's Claude default):_ **Mid-cycle, late innings of
the lock-up phase, pre-recognition** [Manual L1539 inning markers
applied].

The wave is forming. The rate path inflection (Factor 1) has not
fired. The affordability ceiling is binding (Factor 4 + 5
constraints). Layer 2 ratios confirm trough-territory volume + supply
emergence; Layer 3 confirms Mr. Market hasn't fully priced the unwind.

The position is **innings 2-4 of the unlock cycle**. The risk is *not*
that the thesis is wrong; the risk is that pre-recognition extends —
the Fed cut path has stalled, oil is at $80+, and the catalyst
arrival could slip 6-12 months. **Pre-recognition extension is the
load-bearing risk for the next 6-9 months**, not thesis-disconfirmation.

## What confirms recognition is coming

Signal hierarchy [per Manual L1442-1452 Capitulation/Recognition/
Crowding sentiment cycle]:

**Pre-recognition (innings 1-4 — where we are):**
- Sell-side coverage describes the unlock as "uncertain timing"
- Earnings calls describe demand as "soft but stable"
- Apartment REIT 10-Q commentary stays defensive
- "Higher for longer" remains the mainstream framing

**Recognition (innings 5-7):**
- Sell-side coverage flips to "unlock cycle has begun"
- Builder earnings calls flag "sequential demand acceleration"
- Apartment REIT 10-Q commentary reluctantly acknowledges decel
- "Lower rates inevitable" becomes mainstream framing
- Title and brokerage names re-rate up (multiple expansion)

**Crowding (innings 8-9):**
- Sell-side coverage is universally bullish on housing-cycle longs
- Apartment REIT shorts become consensus
- Crowded position; multiple expansion has overshot

**Exit signal (inning 9+):** sentiment is exuberant; the discipline
is to scale out before the unwind, not after.

The current state is **clearly pre-recognition**. The first signal of
recognition will be a Tier-1 builder (DHI, NVR, LEN, PHM) explicitly
flagging "sequential demand acceleration" on a quarterly call —
likely Q3-Q4 2026 if the rate path delivers.

## Counter-thesis on wave position

The strongest version of the bear case on Step 4:

**(1) The trough is structural, not cyclical.** Volume at 4.06M
existing-home sales reflects permanent demand destruction — JCHS
2025's revised 8.6M household forecast for 2025-2035 implies a 10-yr
average existing-home sales pace of perhaps 4.5M, not the 5.3M
2015-2019 average. **What looks like cyclical trough is actually new
mid-cycle baseline.** Returns are smaller and the multiple expansion
is muted.

**(2) Supply emergence outruns demand absorption.** The boomer
demographic wave (Step 2) releases inventory; the affordability
ceiling (Step 2) limits absorption. If supply > demand at any rate
level, the result is *price decline* not volume normalization. Layer
3 valuation expansion doesn't materialize; recognition phase is short
or absent.

**(3) Pre-recognition extends into a different cycle.** The Fed
holds rates higher for longer (oil-driven inflation, Warsh
anti-forward-guidance posture). The unlock catalyst slips from late-
2026 to late-2027. By then the affordability cohort has been further
hollowed out, and the demand absorption mechanism is structurally
weaker than today.

**Counter to counter-thesis.** (1) is partly already in the framework
— Factor 4 bidirectional read acknowledges this risk; the position
sizing reflects the discount. (2) is the load-bearing tension and is
the explicit object of the Step 5 stock-scoring methodology (names
disproportionately serving the affordability-constrained cohort get
discount). (3) is timing, not thesis: a 12-month delay reduces IRR
but doesn't break the structural setup; position-build via the entry-
trigger framework prevents premature concentration.
